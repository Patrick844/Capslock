OpenAI announces new GPT-5 pricing tiers

Published March 28, 2026 on techpress.dev

OpenAI today published a revised pricing schedule for its GPT-5 family of API models, with rates that come in roughly twenty percent higher than the ones in effect since the model launched last fall. The updated schedule covers both GPT-5 standard and GPT-5 Thinking, and the new numbers will apply to all metered API usage beginning in mid-April, with existing enterprise contracts honored at their negotiated terms until renewal.

In the post accompanying the announcement, OpenAI framed the increase as reflecting the true serving cost of GPT-5 after several months of production data, and in particular the significantly higher inference cost of the Thinking variant when customers run it with long reasoning budgets. The company emphasized that per-token pricing for GPT-4 class models is unchanged, and that smaller and older models remain available for workloads where cost sensitivity outweighs capability. Developer reactions on the OpenAI community forum and social channels ran the predictable range, with production teams noting that they will need to revisit their budget forecasts and some indicating they will shift non-critical workloads to cheaper models.

The change lands at a moment when competitors including Anthropic and Google have been holding or cutting prices on their comparable frontier tiers, which makes OpenAI's move a notable directional signal. Analysts at a handful of enterprise-AI advisory firms said the increase is consistent with an industry-wide rebasing of frontier-model economics as adoption shifts from experimentation toward sustained production use. OpenAI confirmed that the mid-April effective date will apply globally and that the company will publish detailed per-region SKU changes in the days ahead.

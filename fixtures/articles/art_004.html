OpenAI adjusts GPT-4 Turbo pricing

Published January 15, 2026 on mlnewsletter.com

OpenAI quietly updated the pricing page for GPT-4 Turbo yesterday, trimming input token costs by roughly fifteen percent and leaving output token pricing unchanged. The adjustment is a modest but noticeable move for the model that still powers a large portion of production LLM workloads, even as newer frontier models have taken over headline benchmarks. The new rates went into effect at the start of the current billing cycle, and existing enterprise agreements with negotiated discounts will be prorated accordingly.

Industry observers read the change as a response to competitive pressure from Anthropic and Google, both of which had shipped price cuts on their mid-tier models in the weeks prior. GPT-4 Turbo has been under gradual margin pressure for a while, as Mixture-of-Experts variants and cheaper distilled models have eaten into its addressable workload. OpenAI's published statement emphasizes that the move "reflects continued efficiency gains in our serving infrastructure" rather than any shift in positioning relative to the GPT-5 family, which remains priced significantly above it.

For developers, the practical effect is small but welcome: a workload that cost about $300 per month under the previous rates will now cost around $255, assuming a typical 70/30 input-to-output token split. Teams running long-context summarization or RAG pipelines, where input tokens dominate, will see the largest benefit. No changes were made to the context window, rate limits, or the model's response latency. OpenAI says a broader pricing review across the API fleet is scheduled for the spring.

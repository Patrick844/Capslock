Deepgram adds real-time diarization

Published February 10, 2026 on voiceaiweekly.com

Speech-to-text provider Deepgram today announced that real-time speaker diarization has left closed beta and is now available across all of its streaming transcription endpoints. The feature, which tags each transcript segment with a stable speaker identifier, has been one of the most frequently requested capabilities on the company's public feedback tracker for more than a year, particularly from teams building meeting summarization, contact-center analytics, and multi-party agent handoff flows.

Until now, Deepgram's diarization has been a post-processing step that only worked reliably on full uploaded audio files. Moving it into the streaming path required the team to rebuild how speaker embeddings are maintained as audio arrives, using a sliding-window attention approach that keeps the identifier assignments stable even when a speaker drops out for several seconds and rejoins. The company says typical diarization error rates sit in the 5 to 8 percent range for two-to-four-speaker calls in English, with broader language support following over the next quarter.

Pricing for the streaming diarization feature is bundled into the existing Nova-3 transcription tier, with no separate per-minute surcharge, which Deepgram positions as a direct shot at a handful of smaller competitors that have historically charged a premium for diarized output. Early users in the launch cohort include a major U.S. telehealth platform and several sales-coaching vendors, both of whom told Deepgram that the ability to run diarization in-line with transcription will let them collapse two pipeline stages into one and cut their end-to-end latency meaningfully.

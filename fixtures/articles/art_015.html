GPT-5 API prices revised: what developers pay now

Published March 29, 2026 on aidaily.io

A day after OpenAI's revised GPT-5 pricing schedule hit the API documentation, developer communities have settled into the work of recalculating their monthly bills. The headline number, confirmed by OpenAI's own pricing page and already circulating in practitioner forums, is an across-the-board rate increase of roughly twenty percent on both GPT-5 standard and GPT-5 Thinking, with the new rates taking effect at the start of the mid-April billing cycle.

For a team running a moderate-volume GPT-5 workload at, say, a blended rate of fifteen dollars per million input tokens and sixty dollars per million output tokens on the previous schedule, the new pricing translates to roughly eighteen and seventy-two dollars respectively. Forum threads on Hacker News and the OpenAI developer forum have been tracking practical break-even points at which it becomes worth shifting specific workloads down to GPT-4 class or open-weight alternatives, and most commenters converged on the view that the increase is survivable but annoying for anyone who has not been actively optimizing their prompt lengths.

The change raises a broader question about where frontier-model pricing is heading. Independent analysts note that Anthropic and Google have been holding the line on their comparable tiers, and that OpenAI's move widens a gap that those vendors are in a position to exploit in multi-vendor procurement cycles. OpenAI has not publicly responded to the competitive framing, though a post on the company's developer blog reiterates that the new rates reflect actual serving costs and emphasizes that the mid-April effective date gives teams several weeks to prepare. Enterprise customers on existing contracts will see the change at their next renewal, not on the flat mid-April flip.

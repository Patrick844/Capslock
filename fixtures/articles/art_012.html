Third-party MCP servers cross 500 in public registry

Published April 5, 2026 on aidaily.io

The community-run public registry of Model Context Protocol servers crossed the 500-entry mark over the weekend, a milestone the registry's maintainers flagged as evidence that MCP has reached a credible level of ecosystem adoption roughly six months after Anthropic's initial draft. The index, which lists self-hosted and third-party vendor-operated MCP servers that connect LLM applications to external tools and data sources, now covers integrations across file storage, project management, calendars, customer-relationship systems, codebases, knowledge bases, and a growing long tail of niche business applications.

Growth over the past quarter has accelerated sharply. The registry reported that the count sat at just over 200 entries in mid-January and has roughly doubled since, driven in part by a wave of first-party servers from well-known SaaS vendors including at least three large CRM platforms and two major cloud-storage providers. Community-maintained servers still account for the majority of listings, and the maintainers say the quality bar is uneven: some entries are production-ready and actively maintained, while others are weekend experiments that have not been touched in months. Work is reportedly underway to add a curation or health signal to the registry to make that distinction legible at a glance.

The practical implication for developers building agents is that the "bring-your-own-tool" problem, which until recently required gluing together bespoke adapters for every integration, is increasingly addressable off the shelf. Several agent frameworks now ship MCP clients as first-class citizens and can consume any conformant server without additional adapter code. Anthropic's own MCP maintainers welcomed the milestone in a short blog post but reiterated that the registry remains community-run and is not an official endorsement of any listed server.

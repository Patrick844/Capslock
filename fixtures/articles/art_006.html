Google Gemini 3 API moves to general availability

Published April 10, 2026 on techpress.dev

Google today flipped the switch on general availability for its Gemini 3 family of models, opening up both Gemini 3 Pro and Gemini 3 Flash to paying Vertex AI and AI Studio customers without a waitlist. After a four-month gated preview that Google used to tune pricing and rate limits, the company is now formally committing to production SLAs on the new line and positioning it as the default replacement for the Gemini 2 family on all new projects.

Gemini 3 Pro leads with what Google describes as a state-of-the-art result on multimodal reasoning benchmarks and a two-million token context window that remains unique among production-grade frontier models. Gemini 3 Flash, the smaller and cheaper sibling, targets workloads where throughput and per-token cost matter more than headline capability, with Google claiming roughly three times the tokens-per-second throughput of the previous Flash generation on equivalent hardware. Both models support native tool use, structured outputs through JSON schema, and the vision and audio inputs that were Gemini 2's calling card.

Pricing at GA sits modestly below the preview rates for the Flash tier and unchanged for the Pro tier, which Google said reflects serving-cost improvements as it has ramped TPU v6e capacity. Developers who were already on the preview will see automatic migration, though a small number of response-format edge cases have changed in subtle ways and Google has published a short migration note. The company also announced that Gemini 3's enterprise tier will include data-residency options in the EU, Japan, and India at GA, something several regulated-industry customers had been explicitly waiting on.

Midjourney v8 enters Discord beta

Published April 12, 2026 on imagegennews.io

Midjourney has opened a Discord-only beta for version 8 of its flagship image model, the company's first major version bump since the v7 general release last summer. The beta is available to existing Midjourney Pro subscribers through the usual /imagine slash command with a new --v 8 flag, and will run in parallel with the v7 default for at least several weeks before any decision is made about promoting v8 to the default generation pipeline.

The headline changes in v8 are tighter prompt adherence at longer prompts and a rebuilt compositional control layer that the team says reduces the number of "near-miss" generations where a complex prompt comes back subtly wrong. Midjourney has historically prioritized aesthetic quality over strict prompt fidelity, and several early testers in the beta cohort described v8 as the first version where those two dimensions feel genuinely balanced. Typography and legible-text rendering, a known weak spot of the Midjourney line, have also seen targeted improvements in v8, though the team is still cautious about how well the gains generalize.

No API access is available during the Discord beta, consistent with Midjourney's long-standing stance that external productization should trail well behind internal quality work. The company reiterated that a formal API is not on the near-term roadmap and that integrations should continue to flow through the Discord surface for now. Pricing for generation during the beta remains bundled into existing Pro and Mega subscription tiers, with no separate metering for v8 images, though the team warned that sustained heavy usage during the beta may be throttled if inference capacity runs tight.

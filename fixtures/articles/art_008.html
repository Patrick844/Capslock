Stable Diffusion 4 released under open-source license

Published March 18, 2026 on imagegennews.io

Stability AI today released Stable Diffusion 4 under a permissive open-source license, a move the company framed as a return to the community-first posture that first made the Stable Diffusion line synonymous with open image generation. The model's weights, architecture details, and training recipes are being published simultaneously on Hugging Face and the Stability AI GitHub organization, with no gated access or tiered commercial terms for the base release.

Stable Diffusion 4 introduces a redesigned rectified-flow sampler that the team says produces comparable or better image quality to the company's previous flagship models in roughly half the steps, while also cutting peak VRAM use on consumer hardware. Stability AI claims that the model can produce a 1024x1024 image on a single consumer-grade GPU with 12 gigabytes of memory in under three seconds at default quality settings. The release also ships with a pair of smaller distilled variants intended for on-device inference on recent mobile chipsets, though these come with reduced prompt fidelity at complex compositions.

Licensing is the most politically watched part of the announcement. Stable Diffusion 4 is being released under an Apache-2.0 license for both the model weights and the accompanying training code, a significant step back toward permissive licensing after the more restrictive terms attached to some of the company's recent proprietary releases. Stability AI's CEO said in an interview that the decision reflects feedback from the open-source community and a strategic bet that ecosystem momentum will compound faster than monetization through license gating. Hosted inference and enterprise support remain available as paid services on the company's commercial platform.

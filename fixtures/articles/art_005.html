Anthropic Claude 4.7 ships with 1M context window

Published April 2, 2026 on aidaily.io

Anthropic today made Claude 4.7 generally available across its API and first-party chat surface, headlined by a one-million token context window that the company says maintains near-baseline recall and reasoning quality even on inputs approaching the full length. The launch closes much of the gap with Google's long-context Gemini family and positions Claude as a credible default for document-heavy enterprise workflows such as legal discovery, financial due diligence, and large codebase analysis.

In Anthropic's own evaluations, Claude 4.7 scored within a point or two of the 200k-token Claude 4.6 baseline on reasoning and coding benchmarks while extending effective retrieval to the far end of the one-million-token window. The company published a needle-in-a-haystack chart showing greater than 99% recall across positions, a noticeable improvement over the degradation typically seen in the last 20% of long-context models. Independent testers at a handful of early-access partners reported that the model is more willing to cite exact spans from source material, which they attributed to additional post-training on long-document grounding tasks.

Pricing for the one-million-token tier sits above Claude 4.6's long-context rate but below what Google charges for comparable Gemini access, reflecting what analysts see as an attempt to win share among the document-processing crowd specifically. Prompt caching, which Anthropic shipped last year, continues to work at the full window length and becomes particularly relevant at this scale, where a single uncached prompt can run into tens of dollars. The company said Claude 4.7 is rolling out to Pro and Team users immediately, with Enterprise availability staged over the coming two weeks.

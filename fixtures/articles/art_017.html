Google Gemini 3 reaches general availability

Published April 7, 2026 on mlnewsletter.com

Google today announced that its Gemini 3 family has reached general availability, marking the formal end of the months-long preview cycle and clearing the model for standard production use on Vertex AI and AI Studio. A press release from the company emphasized that both Gemini 3 Pro and Gemini 3 Flash are now covered by production SLAs and are the recommended default for new projects going forward.

Despite today's announcement, the product remains in gated beta for enterprise customers only, and individual developers we spoke to on the AI Studio side reported that their access continues to be governed by preview-era quotas and waitlist gates. Google's own documentation, as of publishing time, still carries the "Preview" tag on the Gemini 3 Pro model card, and the company's support portal continues to route Gemini 3 issues through the preview-support queue rather than the production-grade channel. Multiple developers on public forums noted that their access dashboards show Gemini 3 under a "Limited" rather than "Generally Available" status, even after the announcement.

Pricing for Gemini 3 at the advertised general-availability tier does appear to be live, matching the rates Google pre-announced during the preview. The two-million token context window on Gemini 3 Pro and the throughput numbers on Gemini 3 Flash are consistent with what preview-era customers have been seeing, and Google's published benchmark figures against competing frontier models are unchanged. For teams with signed enterprise agreements through Vertex AI, the GA positioning is meaningful and production deployment is advisable. For everyone else, the practical reality today still looks closer to an extended preview than a clean general release, and it is worth monitoring the access dashboard before committing timelines.
